Chapter 1: All About Me

Unit 1.1: A New Friend

Let's start by learning how to greet and introduce ourselves. This is a very important basic skill! Pay attention to the dialogue between Galang and Andre on the first day of school.

Galang: Good morning! May I introduce myself? My name is Galang. I'm from Kalimantan. What's your name?
Andre: Good morning! Hi, Galang. My name is Andre. I'm 12 years old and I live here in Jakarta. Nice to meet you.
Galang: Nice to meet you too, Andre. Are you in class 7B?
Andre: Yes, I am. How about you? We are classmates then!

Terjemahan: Mari mulai dengan belajar menyapa dan memperkenalkan diri. Ini dasar yang penting! Perhatikan dialog antara Galang dan Andre di hari pertama sekolah. Galang memperkenalkan dirinya, asalnya, dan bertanya nama Andre. Andre merespon dengan namanya, usianya, dan tempat tinggalnya.

Language Focus: Subject Pronouns & 'to be'

Click to see the explanation ‚ñ∂

We use 'to be' (am, is, are) to describe or identify the subject. It connects the subject with a noun or an adjective.

Penjelasan: Kita menggunakan 'to be' (am, is, are) untuk menjelaskan atau mengidentifikasi subjek. Ini menghubungkan subjek dengan kata benda atau kata sifat.

Pronoun Positive (+) Negative (-) Question (?)

You / We / TheyYou are Andre.We are not late.Are they classmates?
He / She / ItHe is 12 years old.She is not from Bali.Is it your book?

Language Focus: Simple Present Tense & Prepositions of Time

Simple Present Tense (for Routines)‚ñ∂

We use the Simple Present Tense for habits, routines, and general facts.

Aturan Penting: Untuk subjek He, She, It (atau nama orang tunggal), tambahkan -s atau -es pada kata kerjanya.

  • I/You/We/They play basketball.
  • He/She/It plays basketball.
  • I/You/We/They watch TV.
  • He/She/It watches TV.
Prepositions of Time (at, on, in)‚ñ∂
  • at: For specific times (clock times). Contoh: at 5 o'clock, at noon.
  • on: For days and dates. Contoh: on Tuesday, on my birthday.
  • in: For parts of the day, months, years, seasons. Contoh: in the morning, in August, in 2024.

Language Focus: Countable vs Uncountable Nouns

Some nouns can be counted (countable), some cannot (uncountable). This affects how we use quantifiers like 'some' and 'any', and ask 'how many' or 'how much'.

Hover over the bars to see examples. (Arahkan kursor untuk melihat contoh).

How Many vs. How Much‚ñ∂
  • How many + (countable noun jamak): How many meatballs do you want?
  • How much + (uncountable noun): How much soup is in the bowl?

Language Focus: Adjectives & Their Order

Adjective Order‚ñ∂

When you use multiple adjectives, they usually follow a specific order. A simple version is OSASCOMP:

(Urutan Kata Sifat) Saat menggunakan beberapa kata sifat, biasanya ada urutan tertentu.

  1. Opinion (Pendapat): beautiful, nice, cool
  2. Size (Ukuran): big, small, long, short
  3. Age (Usia): old, new, young
  4. Shape (Bentuk): round, square
  5. Color (Warna): black, red, blue
  6. Origin (Asal): Indonesian, Japanese
  7. Material (Bahan): wood, plastic
  8. Purpose (Tujuan): sleeping (bag)

Example: She has a nice (Opinion) small (Size) black (Color) bag.

Chapter 6: Let's Interact

Unit 6.1: Functions of Language

In conversations, we use language for many functions. Look at these examples. (Dalam percakapan, kita menggunakan bahasa untuk banyak fungsi. Lihat contoh-contoh ini).

Asking for Opinion (Meminta Pendapat):

Siti: What do you think about our new classroom?

Dayu: I think it's very clean and comfortable.

Showing Ability (Menunjukkan Kemampuan):

Bima: I can play the guitar, but I can't sing.

Obligation & Prohibition (Kewajiban & Larangan):

Teacher: You must do your homework. You must not be late.

Suggestion & Permission (Saran & Izin):

Monita: You should go to the clinic.

Andre: May I go to the clinic now, Sir?
